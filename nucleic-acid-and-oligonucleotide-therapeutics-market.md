ASOs are designed to bind to specific RNA sequences, altering gene expression to treat diseases. siRNAs silence target genes through RNA interference, providing a mechanism to reduce unwanted protein production. mRNA therapies deliver genetic instructions for cells to produce proteins, offering a new approach for treating various conditions, including genetic disorders and cancers.</p></p>
